Guide to Hosting a Successful Session 0 with Discussion Points

Purpose of Session 0

  • Introduction: Get to know each other and discuss preferences and play style.
  • Expectation Alignment: Set clear expectations for the game and the players' roles.
  • Character Creation: Collaborate on creating characters that fit the campaign and the group.
  • Rule Overview: Review important rules and clarify any questions.

Using Discussion Points

  1. Introduction to Discussion Points

    Explain the purpose of the discussion points as a tool to ensure a good gaming experience for everyone.

    Clarify that they are not accusations but a way to prevent problems.

  2. Discussion Point Review

    Go through each point and discuss what it means for the group.

    • Rule Disregarding: Hvor vigtige er reglerne for jer? Hvordan håndterer vi regler, vi er uenige om?
    • Spotlight Hogging: Hvordan sikrer vi, at alle får lige meget tid i rampelyset?
    • Aggressive Behavior: Hvordan håndterer vi konflikter både i og uden for spillet?
    • Lack of Teamwork: Hvordan opmuntrer vi til samarbejde? Hvad gør vi, hvis en spiller handler alene?
    • Metagaming: Hvad betragter vi som metagaming, og hvordan undgår vi det?
    • Unreliable Participation: Hvordan håndterer vi det, hvis en spiller ofte udebliver?
    • Cheating: Hvilke regler har vi omkring terningkast og karakterark?
    • Disrespect for the Game: Hvordan viser vi respekt for spillet og hinanden?
    • Boundary Violation: Hvad er vores grænser for indhold, og hvordan respekterer vi dem?
    • Conflict Instigation: Hvordan undgår vi bevidst at skabe konflikter i gruppen?
    • Refusal to Engage: Hvad gør vi, hvis en spiller ikke engagerer sig i spillet?
    • Over-Negotiation: Hvordan håndterer vi diskussioner med GM om regler og udfald?
    • Lack of Preparation: Hvilke forventninger har vi til forberedelse før spillet?
    • Disruptive Behavior: Hvordan sikrer vi, at vi holder fokus og ikke distraherer hinanden?
    • Resistance to Feedback: Hvordan giver og modtager vi konstruktiv feedback?
    • Inflexible Roleplaying: Hvordan håndterer vi karakteradfærd, der forstyrrer gruppen?
    • Loot Dominance: Hvordan deler vi loot retfærdigt?
    • Gameplay Sabotage: Hvordan undgår vi at sabotere hinandens spiloplevelse?
    • Lack of Respect for Game Time: Hvordan respekterer vi hinandens tid?
    • Failure to Engage with the Story: Hvordan sikrer vi, at alle engagerer sig i kampagnens historie?

  3. Setting Expectations and Agreements

    Agree on how to handle behavior that may disrupt the game.

    Agree to give and receive feedback constructively.

  4. Prevention of Problems

    Identify potential risks using the discussion points and make a plan to address them.

    Encourage open dialogue and honest communication about any concerns.

Other Important Topics

  • Flexibility and Improvisation: Be prepared to deviate from the book and improvise when players make unexpected decisions.
  • Player Agency: Allow players to influence the story through their choices and actions.
  • Storytelling: Ensure the story is cohesive and engaging.
  • Character Development: Integrate player character backstories into the campaign.
  • Pacing: Balance the pace between combat, roleplaying, and exploration.
  • Atmosphere and Immersion: Use detailed descriptions, music, and sound effects to enhance immersion.
  • Rule Interpretation: Use house rules and be flexible with the rules to ensure a better gaming experience.
  • Challenges and Balancing: Ensure encounters are challenging but not impossible.
  • Expectation Alignment: Set clear expectations for play style and campaign type.
  • Engagement and Preparation:
    • Preparation: Players' responsibility to prepare for sessions, including knowing their characters' abilities and backstories.
    • Active Participation: Encourage active participation in the game, whether in combat scenes or roleplaying situations.

DM's Interpretation of the Adventure Module

  • Freedom: Discuss with players that as a DM, you have the freedom to interpret the adventure module and add or change elements to enrich the story.
  • Engagement: Explain that adding extra elements and making the adventure your own helps keep the DM engaged and passionate about the campaign.
  • Player Expectations: Ensure players are comfortable with the adventure deviating from the written material and that new elements may be added.

Safety Tools

  • X-Card: Introduce a simple safety tool, such as the X-Card, where a player can temporarily pause the game if something uncomfortable happens.
  • Lines and Veils: Discuss boundaries and comfort zones. What are "no-go" topics, and what may be sensitive?
  • Session 0 Agreements: Make agreements about content, comfort zones, and boundaries to ensure everyone's well-being.

Long-Term Goals

  • Campaign Goals: Discuss the overall goals and themes of the campaign to ensure everyone's expectations are aligned.
  • Character Development and Plotlines: Talk about long-term goals for character development and any personal plotlines.

Handling Unforeseen Problems

  • Game Pauses: Agree on procedures for game pauses and how to handle unforeseen issues that may arise.
  • Backup Plans: Have backup plans for sessions where not all players can participate.

Closing Session 0

  • Summary: Summarize the key points and agreements made.
  • Documentation: Document any important points and share them with the group.
  • Open Communication: Encourage ongoing dialogue and feedback throughout the campaign.

Conclusion

A well-conducted Session 0 with the use of discussion points can create a solid foundation for a successful campaign. By identifying and discussing potential problem areas beforehand, you can ensure a fun and positive experience for all participants.
